What should I celebrate today?

2 February 2014

So, the New Year is officially a month ago, its 325 days until Christmas, even the Chinese New Year has been and gone.

The next bank holiday isn’t until 18th April, and the British weather is being its good old predictably unpredictable self.

Depressing, huh?

We thought so too.

But we also thought, the world is a massive place, surely every day is a celebration somewhere in the world?

Thanks to the greatness of Wikipedia (really, they are great, if you can afford it, consider donating), it wasn’t hard to find out. It turns out – someone somewhere in the world is celebrating every day!

See, Wikipedia handily has a page for every date in the Gregorian calander (like this one for today), and down at the bottom it always has a “holidays and observances” section.

It wasn’t to hard to write a little web crawler in Python, that went through every date of the year and pulled out the holidays from that section.

I then did a little bit of culling (for example, there seem to be approximately 100 thousand “Christian Feast Day”s every day – I thought it was unfair giving those guys so much airtime).

With a bit of Python jiggery-pokery to create a JSON file, and an extremely small PHP script, What should I celebrate today? was born.

Go forth and celebrate all the days!

Now you have no excuse, every day can be a celebration as memorable as World Wetlands Day (that’s today).

Other things you might consider celebrating today are:

  • Candlemas

In France it’s also National Crêpe Day. That means pancakes people, lots of pancakes!

  • Celebration of Yemanja

In Salvador, Brazil, thousands of people line up from dawn to give their gifts of flowers, perfume and lipstick to the shrine of Yemanja. Then, there’s a massive street party!

  • Anniversary of the Tartu peace treaty

In Estonia, today is the anniversary of the treaty that ended the Estonian war of independence. Surely there’s no more noble celebration than the end of a war?

Enjoy! Now go and celebrate!
